Sonification of Go Games with Space-filling Curves

Data sonification is the process of translating data into sound and music. It is getting more popular as a method of overcoming literacy, numeracy, and visualization barriers to communications. Sonifying a dataset makes data accessible to a broad audience with different levels of expertise and particularly people with visual impairments. This project designed a method to sonify Go games with space-filling curves. Go is the most challenging classical board game played by two players, which was too difficult for traditional artificial intelligence before AlphaGo. I picked Game 4 between AlphaGo and Lee Sedol (the only game AlphaGo lost) to do the sonification. A space-filling curve is a continuous curve that passes through every point in the unit square. The mapping from one-dimension to two-dimensions does a good job in preserving locality: the points close to each other in 1D are also close to each other in 2D. The Peano curve and Hilbert curve are among the most famous space-filling curves--they are defined on squares with the side length being the power of 3 and 2 respectively. The Go board is 19 by 19, which is neither power of 3 nor power of 2. I first constructed a modified Peano curve, then mapped the Go board coordinates to space-filling curve values. Ultimately, I sonified the data, transforming the Go game into music. In the future, I hope to continue exploring the intersect of different fields through the channel data science.
